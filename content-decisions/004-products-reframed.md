# 004: Products reframed for mixed audience

## Decision

Keep all 6 products but reframe language for a mixed audience of CEOs, IT leads, and non-technical stakeholders.

## Changes

| Original name | Current name | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Build | Build | Clear as is |
| Replace Your SaaS | Build what fits | Less aggressive. "Replace" implies risk. "Build what fits" is aspirational. |
| Performance Audit | Performance Audit | Clear as is |
| Team Upgrade | Team Upgrade | Broadened language beyond dev teams |
| Autopilot for the Boring Stuff | Autopilot for the chores | Slightly more professional while keeping the directness |
| Race Strategy | Race Strategy | Clear as is, ties to racing car analogy |

## Aligned to

`strategy/offering-model.md` product lines, `docs/mission.md` three pillars

## Content rules applied

- Avoid jargon where possible ("codebase" -> "project", "CI pipeline" removed)
- Use "team" not just "developers"
- Descriptions explain value, not implementation

## What we considered but didn't do

- Reducing to 3-4 products. All 6 are distinct enough. Can consolidate later if engagement patterns show overlap.
- Adding pricing hints per product. Too early. Need real engagement data first.
