# Content Decisions

Content decision records for the Performance Dudes website. Each record documents a specific content choice: what we decided, why, and what it aligns to.

## Audience

The website speaks to two readers simultaneously:

1. **CEO / business leader** - cares about budget, speed, risk, independence from vendors
2. **IT lead / engineering manager** - cares about maintainability, team productivity, AI adoption, no lock-in

Both need to feel: "These people are honest, competent, and won't waste my time."

## Content principles

- **Speak about us, not against others.** No competitor bashing. The contrast is implicit.
- **CEO-readable first, technical second.** Top of page: business language. Further down: specifics.
- **Broader than software development.** Dev, ops, security, and non-IT teams.
- **Trust through honesty, not claims.** No vanity metrics. Say what AI can't do, not just what it can.
- **Show the customer journey.** A convinced reader needs to know what happens next.
- **Social proof builds over time.** Honest about being early. Case studies come when they're real.
