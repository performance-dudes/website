# 002: Racing car as the emotional hook

## Decision

The racing car analogy is the second section. It's the brand's signature metaphor: pit crew (us), car (the project), engine (AI), driver (customer's team).

## Why

It's memorable, it explains the relationship clearly, and it sets the tone. After the factual hero, the analogy draws the reader in emotionally.

## Aligned to

`brand/racing-car-analogy.md`

## Changes

- Engine = AI (was "Fuel" in earlier versions). Engine is more central, more essential. Fuel gets burned. An engine is what makes the car go.
- "Codebase" changed to "project" in the mapping. Broader, accessible to non-technical readers.
- Title evolved: "A racing car for your IT" -> "Your IT deserves a pit crew" -> "Built for ambitious drivers". Final version focuses on the customer's ambition, not our service category.
- Removed "sedan stuck in traffic" metaphor for the problem state. Too obscure. Now just describes the reality plainly.

## What we considered but didn't do

- Putting the analogy first, before the hero. Too risky as an opener. Not everyone connects with metaphors immediately.
- Removing the `pre` block with the mapping. Considered too code-like. Kept it because it's visually distinctive and creates a pause in the reading flow.
